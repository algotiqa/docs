# Contributing to the Algotiqa platform

First off, thank you for considering contributing to our trading platform! It’s people like you who make this community great.

To maintain a healthy project and protect both the community and the future of the platform, we have a few guidelines you need to follow before your code can be merged.

## Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Because this project is licensed under the **Elastic License 2.0 (ELv2)** and we plan to offer a commercial SaaS version, we require all contributors to sign our **Contributor License Agreement (CLA)**.

### What does this mean?

You retain the copyright to your code, but you grant us the right to use, modify, and distribute your contributions, including within our commercial SaaS ecosystem.

### How to sign it?

You don't need to do anything manually right now. We use an automated CLA bot:
1. Open your Pull Request (PR) as usual.
2. Our CLA bot will comment on your PR asking you to sign the agreement.
3. Click the link provided by the bot, log in with your GitHub account, and click "Accept".
4. Once signed, the bot will automatically unlock your PR for review.

---

## How to Contribute

### 1. Reporting Bugs
* Check the **Issues** tab to see if the bug has already been reported.
* If not, open a new issue using our bug report template.
* Provide a clear description, steps to reproduce, and any relevant logs.

### 2. Suggesting Enhancements
* Open an issue to discuss your idea before writing any code. This ensures your suggestion aligns with the project's roadmap.

### 3. Submitting Pull Requests
* Fork the repository and create your branch from `main`.
* Ensure your code follows the existing style and formatting of the project.
* Write clear, concise commit messages.
* Make sure all tests pass before submitting.

Thank you again for your time and contribution!
